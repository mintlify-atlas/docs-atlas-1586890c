MoodJourney provides a streamlined interface for capturing your thoughts and emotions through journal entries. Each entry is automatically enhanced with AI-powered sentiment analysis and personalized suggestions.

## Creating a new journal entry

When you first open MoodJourney, you'll see the main journaling interface with a personalized greeting based on the time of day.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start writing">
    Click into the text field and begin typing your thoughts. The editor supports multi-line entries and automatically adjusts to fit your content.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add content">
    Write freely about your day, feelings, or any thoughts you want to capture. There's no minimum or maximum length requirement.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save your entry">
    Click the send icon button (or press Enter while holding Shift) to save your entry. MoodJourney will automatically analyze the sentiment and generate suggestions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Each journal entry is automatically saved with the current date. If you create multiple entries on the same day, the new content will update the existing entry for that date.
</Note>

## What happens when you save

When you save an entry, MoodJourney performs several automated processes:

1. **Emotion classification** - Your text is analyzed using the DistilRoBERTa model to detect emotions (joy, sadness, anger, fear, disgust, surprise, neutral)
2. **AI suggestions** - The Gemini AI generates three personalized suggestions based on your entry content
3. **Entry storage** - Your entry is saved locally with the detected emotion and suggestions appended

```javascript
// Example of what gets saved (from App.jsx:443)
const contentToSave = `${currentEntryText}\n\n🧠 Emotion: ${classifiedEmotion}\n\n💡 Suggestion: ${generatedSuggestion}`;
```

The interface will briefly flash with a color corresponding to the detected emotion, providing immediate visual feedback.

## Viewing your entries

All your journal entries are accessible from the sidebar drawer:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Recent entries">
    The drawer shows your most recent entries by default, displaying the date and a preview of the content.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="View all">
    Toggle "Show All Entries" to see your complete journal history, sorted by date (newest first).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

Click on any entry in the drawer to open it in the main viewing area.

## Editing existing entries

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select an entry">
    Click on an entry from the sidebar to view it in the main area.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter edit mode">
    Click the "Edit Entry" button at the bottom of the entry view.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    Modify the text in the editing area. The editor shows only your original content without the emotion and suggestion annotations.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save changes">
    Click "Save Changes" to update the entry. The emotion and suggestions will be re-analyzed based on your updated content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
When you edit an entry, the emotion classification and AI suggestions are regenerated. Previous analysis results are not preserved.
</Warning>

## Entry format and structure

Each entry consists of:

- **Main content** - Your original journal text
- **Emotion tag** - Automatically detected emotional state
- **AI feedback** - Three suggestions broken down into actionable insights

The emotion and suggestions are displayed separately in the right sidebar when viewing an entry, keeping your original text clean and readable.

## Deleting entries

To remove a journal entry:

1. Select the entry you want to delete
2. Click the "Delete Entry" button (red, with trash icon)
3. Confirm the deletion in the dialog

<Warning>
Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Make sure you want to remove the entry before confirming.
</Warning>

## Adding images to entries

You can attach images to your journal entries for a richer journaling experience:

1. Select an entry from your journal
2. Click the "Upload Image" button
3. Choose an image file from your device (PNG, JPG, etc.)
4. The image will be stored locally and associated with that entry

<Tip>
Once you've added an image, you can use it as a background for that entry's view or enable Monet theming, which extracts colors from the image to customize the interface.
</Tip>

## Entry organization

- **Date-based** - One entry per day, identified by date (YYYY-MM-DD format)
- **Automatic sorting** - Entries are always sorted newest first
- **Persistent storage** - All entries are stored locally on your device in JSON format
